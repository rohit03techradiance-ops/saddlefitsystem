Choosing a Horse Scheme

Use this quick guide to pick the closest match for your horse or riding discipline before running the analysis.

Body Type

  • High-wither: Prominent withers; watch front balance and clearance.
  • Round-barrel: Wide ribcage; check for forward slip and girth stability.
  • Narrow build: Slimmer frame; ensure panel contact and width are adequate.
  • Wide build: Broad back; avoid bridging and excessive rock.
  • Short back: Compact support area; avoid long panels that pass the last rib.
  • Long back: More support area; aim for even contact along the panels.

Discipline

  • Polo / Mounted Archery / Tent Pegging / Racing: Faster motion; allow slightly more rocking and drift.
  • Dressage / Show Jumping / Eventing: Balanced posture is key; be stricter on front-down pitch.
  • Trail / Endurance: Focus on long-duration stability and minimal drift.
  • Reining (Western): Check lateral stability and smooth cadence.
