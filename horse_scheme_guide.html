Choosing a Horse Profile

Pick the closest build match for the horse you are analyzing. This improves front balance, clearance, and saddle stability heuristics without mixing in discipline logic.

High-wither

Use when the withers are prominent and the front of the saddle needs closer clearance monitoring.

Round-barrel

Best for broad ribcages and round backs where girth security and lateral stability matter more.

Narrow build

Good for slimmer horses where panel contact and side-to-side steadiness need extra attention.

Wide build

Use for broad backs where bridging, front roll, and support length deserve more scrutiny.

Short back

Best for compact backs where long panels may extend too far and overload the rear of the saddle.

Long back

Use when there is more support area and the analysis should look for even panel contact over length.

Practical selection rule

  • If the withers are visibly high, start with high_wither.
  • If the ribcage is wide and round, start with round_barrel.
  • If the horse is compact or has limited back length, use short_back.
  • If the exact type is unclear, choose the closest match and review the confidence notes in the report.
